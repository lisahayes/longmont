Southmoor Park is a mid-century Longmont neighborhood built primarily in the 1950s and 1960s on the southern side of the city, extending south of 17th Avenue toward the open land that was still agricultural at the time of its development. The housing stock is single-family Ranch-style and split-level construction with full basements, and the plumbing is a mix of the era: galvanized supply lines in the earliest builds, copper in the homes from the 1960s onward.

Fifty to seventy years of service life puts Southmoor Park's copper supply lines in the pinhole failure window for Longmont's medium-hard St. Vrain surface water. The 1960s copper in particular, installed before municipal water treatment was optimized for corrosion control at pH levels protective of copper, has experienced higher rates of interior pitting than the 1980s copper in newer neighborhoods. St. Vrain water chemistry at the current treatment standard is more protective of copper than it was in the 1960s, but the damage to existing 1960s pipe has accumulated over decades and is not reversible.

What Southmoor Park Homeowners Call About

Pinhole leak detection in 1960s copper supply lines is the most common service request from Southmoor Park. Slab leak detection in the Ranch-style homes with concrete slab foundations (not all Southmoor homes have full basements) follows closely. Water heater assessment is frequent in this neighborhood because the aging supply lines leading to the water heater, particularly the cold inlet, are a failure point that accelerates when the tank's dielectric unions degrade.

The large established trees in Southmoor Park are a sewer lateral risk. Mature cottonwoods and elms planted in the 1950s and 1960s have root systems that have been probing cast iron sewer lateral joints for six decades. A camera inspection of the lateral is the only way to know what the root situation is without excavation.

Southmoor Park Leak FAQ

My 1960s Southmoor Park home has had two pinhole leaks in three years. What should I do?

Two pinholes in three years in a 1960s copper system is a signal that the pipe wall is deteriorating network-wide, not just at the failure points. A whole-house repipe evaluation makes economic sense at that point. We assess adjacent pipe condition after any pinhole repair and give you the honest trade-off between continued spot repairs and a full repipe.

Does my Southmoor Park home have a slab foundation or a basement?

Southmoor Park has both. Ranch-style homes on the southern blocks often have slab-on-grade foundations, which makes slab leak detection the relevant service for supply line failures. Split-levels on the interior streets typically have a partial basement. We confirm the foundation type at first contact and adjust the detection approach accordingly.
