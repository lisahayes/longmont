Longmont Leak Repair Pros provides professional appliance leak detection & repair across Boulder County. Every service call begins with thorough, non-invasive detection to locate the exact failure point before any demolition work begins. We find the leak first.

Why Longmont Homes Need This Service

The St. Vrain Valley's combination of bentonite expansive clay soil, medium-hard surface water from the St. Vrain watershed and C-BT project, and the Front Range freeze-thaw climate creates conditions that stress plumbing across all housing eras. Old Town galvanized lines, 1950s-70s copper in Loomiller and Mill Village, 1980s-90s copper in Spring Valley and Twin Peaks, and even newer PEX systems in Prospect New Town each have their own failure profiles.

Our licensed Colorado plumbers understand these local conditions and tailor the detection method to the specific pipe material, soil context, and neighborhood housing era.

Our Detection Methods

We deploy acoustic listening equipment, thermal imaging cameras, and electronic pressure testing depending on what the situation calls for. For underground lines and slab-embedded pipes, we can also use tracer gas (helium) injection to detect the exit point above the soil surface. The goal in every case is to pinpoint the failure before we break concrete or open drywall.

For sewer and drain lines, a video camera inspection through the clean-out reveals root intrusion, offset joints, and collapsed sections without excavation. We confirm the diagnosis on screen before recommending any repair path.

Repair Options

Once the leak is located, we explain the repair options relevant to your specific situation. For supply lines, options typically include spot repair, line reroute bypassing the damaged section, epoxy lining, and full repipe. For drain lines, options range from spot patching to trenchless repair using pipe-bursting or CIPP lining, which avoids excavation through finished landscaping or basement floors.

We do not push toward the most expensive option. The right repair is the one that addresses the current failure and the realistic remaining life of the adjacent pipe.

How do you detect a appliance leak in Longmont?
